OLD MOON
I reach up to the moon
With one pale hand
And realize that we are not attuned
I step into my hole
Then carefully stand
To spare my mud wall etchings: they’ll dry soon

I bend to ingest salts
Then eye my glyphs
And feel new neurotransmitters fill faults
I’ve drawn things to control
A God whose cliffs
Are more than fractured limestones and basalts

I know my moon God errs
But I still pray
Ordering tiny soldier Egyptians
To line up crown to sole
That ancient way
Until, in Rah’s tall tales, my figure wins

Now groans the moon’s dark side
That I can’t see
Though others from another earth have spied
The crafted circle’s toll
On Gods and me
Imperfect beings weakened as they’ve lied

Anubis, now I start
On muddied bed
So crowded I can’t draw his legs apart
From my still hero’s stroll
A jackal’s head
Now on my own neck, weighing my own heart

An honorable fear
Is not a boon
Unlike a crescent made of newness near
Which negates the black soul
Of an old moon
I crawl up to view my new God from here

DANCING WITH MY GHOSTS
Walked further than I usually do
On ushering of a ghoulish man
Night promenade towards windows through
Which I would gaze a staged élan
The hall was dressed with ambers, brown
But empty… ‘cept then I did find
A girl who held up one coal crown
To ashen dreams did bow my mind
Perhaps the idea of a ghost
Accepting offered arm on street
Was too expectant of this host
But hadn't she foretold we'd meet?
When I was young, oh Natalie
She lived not near, but miles away
I held her then returned to see
If she’d appear there the next day
So summers of my youth are gone
These winters of my passing years
Are darker than that late June lawn
A clearing made of woodened fears
I'm on a bus but barely there
She’s sirened sorry, spectrally!
Mind floats away to court its pair
Rejecting old dichotomy
Left suit and polished shoes accept
New invitation perfumed so
They navigate down steps inept
To watch another’s bones regrow
The caller rises from a soil
Compressing, sounds of xylophone
The smell of whiskey, wet and toil
Its jaw contorts to smile, groan
This figure lies to body’s ears
And moves towards eyes that will not see
The sooty queen who curtsies years
Nor, now a mother, Natalie
My ballroom wasn't ever grand
Too touched by misery so fine
I outstretch both a blackened hand
And dance with these three ghosts of mine

UNTITLED
The planets, obeying their internal laws, don't touch
I wanted to think about what this meant

So on with my space suit
Through visor:
people who admit their true feelings

Ignoring the echo of automatic organs
See id vs. superego, matter vs. antimatter
Notepad held just above my glass stomach
I wrote "Sweetness, sweetness everywhere."

Writing questions:
"Does an eclipse count?" and "Have you looked at a snake recently?"
And "Don't any of the stars form a snake?"

Writing instructions:
"All comes from nothing." and "Accept that your perfection is doomed."
And "Imagine the near future until you're mad." and "What will come has already come."
